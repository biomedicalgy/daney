An X-ray machine is any machine that involves X-rays. It may consist of an X-ray generator and an X-ray detector. Examples include: Machines for medical projectional radiography Machines for computed tomography Backscatter X-ray machines, used as "body scanners" in airport security Detectors in X-ray astronomy In 1909, Mónico Sánchez Moreno invented the first portable x-ray machine

created by... How an X-ray machine works: The modern x-ray machine is simply put, a forced cathode emitter. Although there are many differences between x-ray systems, the basics are actually the same. A charge of electrons is developed on the cathode of an x-ray tube. When the desired level of charge is reached, a positive potential is applied to the anode causing the electrons to jump at high velocity from cathode to anode. When those electrons suddenly decelerate by impacting the surface of the anode, energy is released in the form of x-rays I hope this helps; Dale Callaway
